We study the genomics of DNA damage, repair, and mutagenesis in pediatric cancer and in response to therapy. Using a combination of computational biology, machine learning, and molecular biological methods we try to understand the complexity of the underlying mechanisms. The aim is on the one hand to prevent pediatric cancer, where possible, and on the other hand to use deregulated mechanisms to improve treatment strategies. Finally, we try to understand currently used chemo- and radiotherapy better in order to prevent long term adverse side effects.

The group has recently started out at St.Anna-Childhood Cancer Research Institute in Vienna. Within pediatric cancers we are focussing mainly on genome instability in neuroblastoma, Ewing's sarcoma and pediatric brain tumours, and the associated therapies.
